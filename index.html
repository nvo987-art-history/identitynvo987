Infrastructure Overview

The decentralized digital identity node associated with the identifier did:web:identity.nvo987.us.

This website serves as the central reference point on the internet for the personal research identity and digital infrastructure of Nicholas Van-Orton. The purpose of the system is to establish a stable, long-term referencable identity that connects research activity, publications, cultural projects, and related digital resources.

Nicholas Van-Orton works as an independent researcher without an institutional affiliation to a university or research institute. His work primarily focuses on modern and contemporary visual culture, digital humanities, as well as the technological and cultural questions of decentralized digital identity and the semantic web.

The identity infrastructure is based on open web standards, including Decentralized Identifiers (DID), JSON-LD, FOAF, RDF, RSS and Atom metadata formats. These technologies make it possible for the personal research profile, publications and related knowledge graph structures to be accessible in a machine-readable and interoperable form on the decentralized web.

The goal of the system is to create an open and interconnected research and cultural knowledge network that supports the authentication of digital identity, the stable identification of scholarly references, and the long-term archiving of research outputs.

Researcher Profile

Nicholas Van-Orton

Independent researcher working in the fields of decentralized digital identity, the semantic web, and knowledge representation. His research focuses on the Civilizational Interpretive Ratio model, which examines the relationship between the complexity of autonomous systems and human interpretive capacity, as well as on the role of color as a generative principle in the work of Sonia Delaunay and in the context of algorithmic image generation.

Founder and President of the cultural and research association NVO987 – Culture Visuelle Moderne et Contemporaine.
